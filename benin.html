Welcome to Benin

History

The Republic of Benin was formed in 1960 after gaining independence from France. Following its independence, the vacuum of power left after the French's departure produced a succession of military coups between 1960 and 1972. Major Mathieu Kérékou ended the military coups on the 26th of October, 1972, and rule for the next 19 years. During this time, Benin's government followed Marxist-Leninist principles which saw numerous executions of political opponents, the paralyzing of the economy, and an increase in external debt. In 1977, mercenary Bob Denard led a failed coup against President Kérékou later called Operation Shrimp.

By 1986, the economic system, as a result of President Kérékou's policies, had plunged into a critical state. To alleviate the economic problems faced by Benin, the country signed a deal with the International Monetary Fund(IMF) in 1987. The IMF imposed a 10% additional levy on wages, hiring freezes, and mandatory retirements for government employees. While Benin received the loan, strikes from students and staff were held around the country due to having been cut off from their funding. With no money to pay the army, these riots continued. The Benin banking system eventually collapsed in 1989. Realizing his Marxist-Lenin policies could not bring his country out of the nadir he had created, he moved toward establishing Benin as a democratic country. In its first election, Benin chose Nicéephore Soglo as president, which gave end to President Kérékou's 19 year reign over the country.

Today's current president of Benin is Patrice Talon. Talon's current mission is to reform his country's constitution, specifically limiting a president to a single term of five years, and to reduce the size of the goverment.
