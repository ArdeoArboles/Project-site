Welcome to Orcadas Laboratory in Antartica!!

History

At the beginning of 1903, a Scottich expedition led by William Speirs Bruce sailed to the Artic Sea While exploring the coast of Orcadas del Sur, their boat became trapped in the ice near the Laurie Island. So as to avoid freezing to death, the crew made a makeshift house, later to be called the Omond House, and waited for the ice trapping their boat to melt.

Finally, in December of 1903, the crew's patience had paid off as the ice which "imprisoned" the boat had melted. Realizing the crew's supplies were running low, he took the opportunity to sail to Buenos Aires and restock. He left his meteorologist Robert Mossman in charge to continue the research while he set sail for Argentina. Upon arrival in Argentina, Bruce offered to sell the Argentinian government the Omond House and the research instruments they used for $5.000 pesos, with the provision that Argentina continue the crew's scientific research. Argentinian president Julio Argentino Roca submitted the proposal to the Oficina Meteorologica Nacional(National Office of Meteorology) and received approval to accept Bruce's proposal. On the 2nd of January, 1904, the Argentinian government ratified the proposal and Bruce's installations passed hands to the Argentinians.

Getting Around


If you are lucky enough to be invited to do research, you will have the opportunity to traverse the base using a Snowcat, a 6x6, a snowmobile, or a small Zodiak boat. Just make sure to stay WARM! The annual average temperature is -4.2 ℃(24 ℉)
